Essential Appliance Maintenance Tips

Extend the life of your home appliances with these expert maintenance tips.

Keep Your Refrigerator Running Efficiently

Regularly clean the condenser coils at the back or bottom of your fridge to prevent dust buildup, which can cause the compressor to overwork and increase electricity bills. Ensure that door seals are tight by performing the dollar bill test—if it slides out easily, the seals need replacing.

Clean Your Washing Machine Monthly

Run an empty cycle with white vinegar to remove detergent residue, followed by a baking soda cycle to eliminate odors. Wipe down the rubber door gasket and leave the lid open between washes to allow proper ventilation and prevent mold growth.

Avoid Overloading Your Dishwasher

Overloading the dishwasher can block water spray and reduce cleaning efficiency. Ensure optimal performance by spacing out dishes properly and cleaning the filter monthly. Running an empty cycle with vinegar helps dissolve grease and mineral deposits.

Maintain Your Oven and Stove

Use a non-abrasive cleaner for glass cooktops, and clean gas burners with warm, soapy water. Wipe down spills immediately to prevent buildup. If you have a self-cleaning oven, follow the manufacturer’s guidelines carefully to avoid damaging internal components.

Final Thoughts

Consistent maintenance of your appliances not only extends their lifespan but also improves efficiency and saves you money on repairs. Make these simple upkeep routines a habit, and your appliances will serve you well for years to come.
